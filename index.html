Problem

Distance changes exposure faster than intuition.

Moving a flash farther away does more than make the light look smaller in the room. Exposure falls with the square of the distance, so small setup changes can cost multiple stops.

Distance x2 1/4 light add x4 power, or +2 stops
Distance x4 1/16 light add x16 power, or +4 stops
Distance x0.5 x4 light reduce power by two stops

Product

One lab, three linked views.

Architecture and features

A small static app with transparent behavior.

The site runs in the browser with HTML, CSS, and JavaScript. The calculation is intentionally visible through the formula, readouts, and stop meter.

Formula

power / distance2

Exposure at the subject is calculated from flash power and distance squared.

Beam

30-degree angle

The visual beam keeps a consistent angle while distance changes.

Range

2 ft to 16 ft

The distance slider covers common close studio distances for learning falloff.

Power

1/128 to 1/1

Full-stop flash settings mirror common manual strobe controls.
